## [LONG] Explain the difference between mitosis and meiosis.

> Mitosis is a type of cell division that produces two identical daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the parent cell. It is used for growth, repair, and asexual reproduction. The process involves one division cycle with stages: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase.
>
> Meiosis, on the other hand, is a type of cell division that produces four genetically unique daughter cells, each with half the number of chromosomes as the parent cell. Meiosis occurs in two division cycles (meiosis I and meiosis II) and is essential for producing gametes (sex cells). It introduces genetic variation through crossing over during prophase I and independent assortment of chromosomes.
>
> The key differences are: mitosis produces 2 identical cells while meiosis produces 4 unique cells; mitosis maintains chromosome number while meiosis halves it; mitosis is for growth while meiosis is for reproduction.

## [SHORT] What is the difference between an element and a compound?

> An element is a pure substance made of only one type of atom, while a compound is a substance made of two or more different types of atoms chemically bonded together in fixed proportions.
